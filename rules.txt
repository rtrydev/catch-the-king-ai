Catch the king is a card game, which aim is to collect as many points as possible.
There is a board consisting of a grid of 5x5 cards that have values from 1 to 5 and K being the king.
The cards are not shown to the player at first, and are uncovered during the gameplay.
The player has the following cards in their hand: 5x[1], 2x[2], 2x[3], 1x[4], 1x[5], 1x[K].
You always start with the lowest card.
25 Hidden cards are on the table.

Gameplay:
When you click on a card on the field, the value is compared to that of your own:
    - If your card is greater, you will receive points equal to the value of the revealed card and you will keep the card, meaning you can turn over another card on the board.
    - If the cards are the same value, you receive the points and your turn is over. Play continues with a new card from your deck, that means if you have the last [1] card in hand it immediately changes to the [2].
    - If your card’s value is lower, you don’t receive any points. The card is hidden again (the value stays the same), and can be interacted with again. Your turn is over and play continues with a new card.
    - If after the card is chosen and 5 is present somewhere among the 8 neighbouring fields, these 8 fields will be highlighted.
    - If your card is a 5 and there is a face-down (not revealed) 5 among the 8 neighbouring fields, your card will be captured. Your turn is over and you don’t receive any points.
    - If your card is the king (K) and you reveal the king on the board, you receive 100 points. Any other card revealed will not give you points and the game is over.
    - If you reveal all cards in a row or column, you receive 10 extra points.

There are the following cards on the board:
[1]: 7 cards
[2]: 4 cards
[3]: 5 cards
[4]: 5 cards
[5]: 3 cards
[K]: 1 card

Cards and their values:
[1]: 10 points
[2]: 20 points
[3]: 30 points
[4]: 40 points
[5]: 50 points
[K]: 100 points

There are two score thresholds: 400 points or more - silver, 550 points or more - gold.
When the hint fields are revealed, they don't stay like this - of course a cautious player can note which fields were revealed.
The hint disappearing creates a possibility to pinpoint the locations of [5] cards because from one angle the hint appears and from another it doesn't - thus by elimination it is possible to know where the 5 is.
